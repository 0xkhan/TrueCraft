Physics engine rewritten, now less buggy

Published Jul 07, 2015

If you did play with mobs since they were implemented a few days ago, you probably noticed how janky they were. This was a result of the old physics engine from Craft.Net being carried forward into TrueCraft - but as of today, said physics engine has been thrown out and rewritten from scratch.

Mobs should no longer behave so strangely. We'll also soon see improvements in how the client handles physics, since the server and client can share the physics engine now. However, due to a special case with how Minecraft handles item and sand/gravel entities, both will be buggy for the time being.

There have also been some important optimizations done. Lighting was recently disabled due to being a huge drain on the server's resources, but I spent some time finding the bottlenecks and reducing the time required to perform lighting operations significantly, and it should no longer be an issue.

Additionally, when chunks are loaded, they are now given a chance to schedule updates for themselves, which means that your crops will continue to grow after a server restart. As I implemented that, I fleshed out grass blocks so that they'd spread to nearby dirt blocks, and die when opaque blocks are placed on top of them.

I'm going to be focusing on optimizations for a while, so for the near future you should just expect to see the performance getting better rather than any groundbreaking features being added.
